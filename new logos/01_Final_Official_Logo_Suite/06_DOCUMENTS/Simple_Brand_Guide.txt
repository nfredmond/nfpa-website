Nat Ford Planning — Simple Brand Guide

1. Core Brand Structure
-----------------------
Primary logo:
Use the stacked logo as the main official brand mark.

Secondary logo:
Use the horizontal logo for headers, signatures, and wide placements.

Primary monogram:
Use the square monogram as the main icon/logo symbol.

Alternate monogram:
Use the circular monogram as an alternate badge or seal.

Monochrome:
Use the monochrome versions where a simple one-color mark is needed.

2. Preferred Usage
------------------
Primary stacked logo:
- website hero area
- proposals
- presentations
- signage
- premium printed materials

Horizontal logo:
- website headers
- email signatures
- social covers
- footers
- banner placements

Square monogram:
- favicon
- social profile image
- watermark
- app/icon use
- small-space branding

Circular monogram:
- decorative seal
- premium collateral
- alternate social icon
- specialty branding

Monochrome versions:
- embossing
- engraving
- foil
- invoices
- stationery
- low-ink or single-color printing

3. Light vs Dark Rules
----------------------
Light versions:
Use on white or very light backgrounds.

Dark versions:
Use on black, charcoal, or dark-colored backgrounds.

Do not place the light logo on dark backgrounds unless it is the dark-mode version.
Do not place the dark logo on light backgrounds unless contrast remains excellent.

4. Brand Colors (approximate)
-----------------------------
Brand Black: #111111
Signature Gold: #B8934E
White: #FFFFFF

5. Spacing / Clear Space
------------------------
Keep generous clear space around all logos.
As a simple rule, leave at least the height of the “N” stem in visual breathing room around the mark whenever possible.

6. Minimum Size Guidance
------------------------
Primary stacked logo:
Avoid using it too small. Best for medium and large placements.

Horizontal logo:
Best when width is available. Good for smaller header applications.

Monogram:
Best for very small applications such as favicons, profile icons, and watermarks.

7. What Not To Do
-----------------
Do not stretch the logo.
Do not rotate the logo.
Do not change the letter order.
Do not recolor the logo arbitrarily.
Do not place busy imagery directly behind the logo.
Do not crowd the logo with other elements.
Do not recreate the monogram with the F in front of the N.

8. Official Recommendation
--------------------------
Official primary mark:
Square-monogram stacked logo

Official secondary mark:
Horizontal lockup

Official icon:
Square monogram

Official alternate:
Circular monogram
